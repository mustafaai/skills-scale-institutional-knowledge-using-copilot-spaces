# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designers

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers research user needs, create intuitive interfaces, and ensure consistency and accessibility across product experiences. They collaborate with Product Managers, Developers, and stakeholders to balance user goals with technical and business constraints.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics
- Ensure accessibility (WCAG) and responsive design standards

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, intuitive experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction metrics
- Maintain design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with Product and Engineering
- User research findings and recommendation reports
- Design specs and handoff documentation via Figma or similar tools
- Regular syncs with developers during implementation

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs, prioritize design work, and validate designs against success metrics
- **Developers**: Collaborate on technical feasibility, provide design specs, and review implementation
- **Data Analysts**: Review user behavior data to inform design decisions
- **Project Managers**: Provide design effort estimates and flag design-related risks

---

## Data Analysts

### Role Summary
Data Analysts collect, analyze, and interpret data to inform decision-making across product, engineering, and business teams. They create dashboards, reports, and insights that drive strategy and measure success.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Build and maintain dashboards and reporting tools
- Conduct data analysis to identify trends and opportunities
- Collaborate with Product and Engineering on instrumentation and tracking
- Present insights and recommendations to stakeholders
- Ensure data quality and governance standards

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across the organization
- Improve visibility into product performance and user behavior
- Identify opportunities for optimization and growth

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews with Product and Engineering leads
- Ad-hoc analysis requests and findings presentations
- Dashboard and report sharing via BI tools
- Data requirements documentation for new features

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Define success metrics, analyze feature performance, and provide insights for prioritization
- **UX/UI Designers**: Share user behavior data to inform design decisions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on instrumentation, event tracking, and data pipeline requirements
- **Project Managers**: Provide project-level metrics and progress tracking data

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and oversee the release process, ensuring features and fixes are deployed safely, on schedule, and with minimal disruption. They manage release planning, coordination, and communication across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release schedules and timelines
- Coordinate release planning across multiple teams and dependencies
- Manage release branch strategy and version control processes
- Oversee pre-release validation (testing, security scans, documentation)
- Lead go/no-go decisions for production deployments
- Coordinate rollback procedures when needed
- Maintain release documentation and post-mortems

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, predictable release cadence
- Minimize production incidents and deployment risks
- Maintain clear communication and coordination across all stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with Engineering and Product leads
- Daily release status updates during active release windows
- Release notes and deployment notifications
- Post-release reports and incident reviews

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Align on project timelines and milestone dependencies
- **Developers**: Coordinate code freezes, branch management, and deployment readiness
- **Support Lead**: Provide release notes and prepare support team for new features
- **Product Managers**: Validate feature completeness and prioritize release contents

---

## Support Leads

### Role Summary
Support Leads manage customer support operations, ensure timely issue resolution, and serve as the voice of the customer within the organization. They identify patterns in customer issues and advocate for fixes and improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Manage support ticket queues and SLA compliance
- Triage and escalate critical customer issues
- Maintain support documentation and knowledge base
- Identify recurring issues and advocate for product improvements
- Train and onboard support team members
- Coordinate with Engineering on bug fixes and incident response
- Collect and share customer feedback with Product teams

### Goals
- Maintain high customer satisfaction and resolution times
- Reduce repeat issues through advocacy for systemic fixes
- Ensure support team has knowledge and tools to be effective

### Typical Communication
- Daily triage meetings with support team
- Weekly syncs with Engineering on open issues and bugs
- Monthly reporting on support metrics and trends
- Customer communications during incidents and escalations

### Key Interactions
- **Release Managers**: Review release notes and prepare for post-deployment support
- **Product Managers**: Share customer feedback and pain points to inform roadmap
- **Developers**: Collaborate on bug reproduction, triage, and resolution
- **Project Managers**: Flag support-impacting issues and participate in incident response

## Role Review Checklist

Use this checklist periodically (e.g., quarterly or after major organizational changes) to ensure roles and responsibilities remain clear and up-to-date:

- [ ] All active roles in the organization are documented
- [ ] Each role has clear responsibilities and goals defined
- [ ] Role interactions and collaboration patterns are described
- [ ] Communication channels and cadences are up-to-date
- [ ] Responsibilities do not significantly overlap or create gaps
- [ ] New team members can use these definitions for onboarding
- [ ] Roles align with current project management processes and tools
- [ ] Each role's success metrics are measurable and tracked
- [ ] Stakeholders have reviewed and validated role definitions
- [ ] Documentation reflects any recent organizational or process changes
